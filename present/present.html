01 — Introduction

Introduction to Time-Varying Fields

Definition

Time-varying fields are electric (E) and magnetic (H) fields whose magnitude and direction change as a function of time.

The Source

  • Static fields are created by stationary charges or steady direct currents (DC).
  • Time-varying fields are generated by accelerating charges or alternating currents (AC).
Dev Analogy If a static field is like a plain HTML page that never changes once it loads — a time-varying field is like a dynamic, real-time application using WebSockets in a React/Node.js environment. It is constantly updating, driving changes, and interacting dynamically with its surroundings.

02 — Comparison

Static vs. Time-Varying Fields

Feature Static Fields Time-Varying Fields
Source Charges at rest, steady currents (DC) Accelerating charges, alternating currents (AC)
Relationship Decoupled. Electric and magnetic fields exist independently. Coupled. A changing E creates H, and vice versa.
Energy Localized in space around the charge/current. Propagates through space as electromagnetic waves.
Governing Rules Coulomb's Law, Biot-Savart Law Maxwell's Equations
03 — Physical Meaning

Physical Meaning of Changing E and H

Changing H → Creates E  ·  Faraday's Law

A changing magnetic field forces electrons to move — inducing an electromotive force (voltage). Changing the magnetic flux through a loop of wire induces that voltage.

\[\nabla \times \mathbf{E} = -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t}\]

B is the magnetic flux density, directly related to H.
The negative sign represents Lenz's Law — the induced field opposes the change that created it.

Changing E → Creates H  ·  Maxwell-Ampere Law

A changing electric field across empty space (like between capacitor plates) acts like a physical current — called displacement current.

\[\nabla \times \mathbf{H} = \mathbf{J} + \frac{\partial \mathbf{D}}{\partial t}\]

\(\frac{\partial \mathbf{D}}{\partial t}\) is the displacement current density, representing the changing electric field.

04 — Applications

Application in Electrical Machines

⚡
Transformers

Step AC voltage up or down using time-varying fields.

  1. Alternating current flows into the primary coil.
  2. AC creates a time-varying magnetic field (H) in the iron core.
  3. Changing field travels through the core to the secondary coil.
  4. Faraday's Law induces a time-varying E, generating AC voltage in the secondary coil.
⚙️
Induction Motors

Convert electrical energy into mechanical movement.

  1. Multi-phase AC power feeds into the stator.
  2. Creates a time-varying, rotating magnetic field.
  3. Rotating field cuts across the bars of the rotor.
  4. Faraday's Law induces electric current in the rotor.
  5. Induced current + magnetic field → Lorentz force → torque → rotation.
